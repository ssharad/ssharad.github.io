Short Bio

I am a first-year graduate student pursuing M.Sc. in Computer Science at TU Delft, the Netherlands. I hold a B.Tech. in Computer Science and Engineering from Amrita University, India. My research focuses on building smart ecosystems combining the Internet of Things with Machine Learning and Data Science. In the information age, there are different sources around us that generate data. I develop platforms and devices that can log, visualize and take decisions based on such data.

I started research on this topic from the third year of my undergraduate study. I have developed solutions for energy-monitoring, smart transport, and real-time safety systems combining IoT with Machine Learning.

My current research ranges from design and development of better communication protocols for IoT to implementing fast algorithms to perform search and data manipulation on big data.

My passion is to creatively invent new, robust, cost-effective digital platforms to solve challenging problems.

In my spare time, I enjoy listening to music (Instrumental, Indian Classical fusions, Cover songs) and meditate. I write answers on Quora in topics related to Internet of Things and other random topics. Through my blog , I share interesting things that I encounter while learning new things.

Research Intern

During this internship, I used Texas Instruments' CC3200 launchpad as a sensor node and connected it to the internet using the Message Queueing Telemetry Transport (MQTT) protocol. The data acquired from the sensor node was stored on MySQL database. I also developed a mini-web application that uses Google Charts API to visualize the data acquired. This sensor node is developed as part of the "Wireless Sensor Network enabled earlier real-time detection of spoilage in stored grains" projected funded by the Dept. of Science and Technology, Govt. of India. under the DST Reference No.: IDP/IND/22/2012-G Dt.:25.09.2013.
