About Me

I am at present pursuing my Masters in Computer Science at the Delft University of Technology in the Netherlands.

Prior to starting my master's program at TU Delft, my research as a student at Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham in India was in the development of building smart ecosystems combining the Internet of Things with Predictive Analytics. The research projects focused on the energy and transportation sectors and can be found in the proceedings of ACM e-Energy, IEEE ANTS, and Procedia Computer Science.

After coming to TU Delft, my research interests have broadened to span across Crowd Computing, Data Science, Machine Learning along with the Internet of Things. During my masters, my research focuses using on Urban Data Science for Smart Cities with the Internet of Things and Crowd as data sources.

I either write or listen to music or binge watch animes and seldom take long walks to divert myself from studies and work.

Recent Work/Research Experience

Undergraduate Student Research Associate Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, Coimbatore (2015 - 2016)

Combined Locality Sensitive Hashing with Big Data Environments of Hadoop and Apache Spark to search large log files of network packet data faster. The implemented algorithm was faster than conventional algorithms used to perform search on large datasets.
This work was carried out as part of the Malware Analysis and Detection using Sandboxing and Machine Learning project funded by IBM Shared University Research program.

Undergraduate Student Researcher - Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham, Coimbatore (2014 - 2016)

I did most of my undergraduate research during this time in the domain of Internet of Things and Predictive Analytics. My responsibilities were to design and develop scalable prototypes for real world problems in energy and transportation sectors. The core design principle I used was to employ Internet of Things for data acquisition and Predictive Analytics or Machine Learning algorithms for decision-making.
I was also engaged in conducting workshop and seminar on Beaglebone Black(2015) and GPS for IoT(2016). All the projects I have worked on here are converted into research publications that are published as conference proceedings of ACM e-Energy 2015, IEEE ANTS 2016 (to appear) and a journal article in Elsevier's Procedia Computer Science (2016).

Summer Research Intern - Velammal College of Engineering, Chennai (2014 - 2016)

Sensors play an important role in the Internet of Things space especially for developing solutions to real world problems. During this internship, I used Texas Instruments' CC3200 launchpad as a sensor node and connected it to the internet using the Message Queueing Telemetry Transport (MQTT) protocol. The data acquired from the sensor node was stored on MySQL database. I also developed a mini-web application that uses Google Charts API to visualize the data acquired.
This sensor node is developed as part of the ​"Wireless Sensor Network enabled earlier real-time detection of spoilage in stored grains" projected funded by the Dept. of Science and Technology, Govt. of India.
