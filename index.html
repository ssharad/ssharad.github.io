My Bio

My name is Sharad Shriram. I am pursuing my Masters in Computer Science at the Delft University of Technology in Delft, the Netherlands. Prior to this, I received my Bachelor of Technology in Computer Science and Engineering from Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham in Coimbatore, India.

I am tinkerer, developer, dreamer and the occassional wirter. These days I am stumbling upon and new things in my masters education and building some really exciting projects (will be updated soon). My interest and motivation is to build technology that uses the different data sources around us in our lives and use that data to improve our lifestyle. The interest in Data Science for me is still nascent which makes me curious to learn new concepts like crowd computing, etc. My design goal is to bring the "human" factor in the digital world and I dream of bringing a human-machine co-existence with the human being at the center.

My writings are mostly in the form of answers on Quora. The other one is the technical blog on Wordpress called the jounral of the geek which is updated aperiodically.

When not occupied, I either write or listen to music or binge watch animes and seldom take long walks to divert myself from studies and work.




For information about my professional background, please take a look at my Linkedin profile or my CV.

Research

My perception of research is a way of expressing the future of technology that one finds interesting.

Prior to starting my master's program at TU Delft, my research as a student at Amrita Vishwa Vidyapeetham in India was in the development of building smart ecosystems combining the Internet of Things with Predictive Analytics. The research projects focused on the energy and transportation sectors and can be found in the proceedings of ACM e-Energy, IEEE ANTS, and Procedia Computer Science.

After coming to TU Delft, my research interests have broadened to span across Crowd Computing, Data Science, Machine Learning along with the Internet of Things. During my masters, my research focuses using on Urban Data Science for Smart Cities with the Internet of Things and Crowd as data sources.
